    • Sarcoma is a soft tissue cancer that mainly develops in tissues like fat, muscle, nerves, fibrous tissues, or blood vessels. This cancer is able to be found in any part of the body, but it most commonly is found in the arms and legs of a person. This doesn't mean that it cannot be found in other areas of the body. Ex: trunk, head and neck area, internal organs, and the area in back of the abdominal (belly) cavity (known as the retroperitoneum). While the term is a broad categorization of many different cancers, it is much easier to group them together instead of focusing on just one specific type of cancer.

    Symptoms

    The symptoms include:
  • Sudden lump growing in the skin, wich may or may not be painful
  • Bone pain
  • Unexpected broken bone, caused by minor or no injury at all
  • Abdominal pain
  • Wight loss

    There may be other symptoms of Sarcoma cancers, but the most persistant ones are these main 5.

    Is it Serious?

    Sarcoma cancers are considered a quite serious cancer, and typically only cureable if treated before it spreads. 81% of adults treated during this time period, are alive 5 years after treatment If a person is treated after the cancer has already spead, the chance of living after 5 years is sitting at a low 65%.
